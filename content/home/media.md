My research is motivated by questions of direct policy relevance, and I maintain active partnerships with governments and international organizations to ensure that it is.

**Humanitarian aid targeting.** I co-led a six-year engagement with UNHCR Lebanon (2018–2024) in the assessment, design, analysis, and implementation of the cash transfer targeting system for Syrian refugees. The econometric model my coauthors and I developed has been put directly into practice to allocate over $1 billion in humanitarian aid. I led a similar engagement with UNHCR Iraq (2023–24), and am currently involved in multiple projects with IOM Iraq analyzing the effects of livelihoods programming on social cohesion.

**Skills training and labor market policy.** As Principal Investigator for a study of Brazil's national skills training program (PRONATEC), I presented results to the Special Secretariat for Strategic Affairs of the Brazilian Presidency and the Ministry of Industry. This work was also presented in a hearing of the Brazilian Federal Senate and informed subsequent policy discussions on technical and vocational training reform at the federal level.

**World Bank.** I served as Lead Specialist and Principal Investigator at the World Bank (2016–2018) and as a consultant (2010–2016) on private-sector development and poverty research. I have been asked to review more than $14 million in grants across more than 30 proposals for the World Bank and other international organizations, as well as National Science Foundation grant funding proposals.

**Fieldwork and country experience.** I have consulted for or collaborated with the World Bank, UNHCR, WFP, IOM, national governments and ministries, and local and international NGOs across India, Brazil, Lebanon, Jordan, Iraq, and Egypt.
